## THE OWNER'S LAW (the model, restated once)
Each year ~100 players enter; we know THAT they come, not WHO — so the known entry slots are
credited at the PVC of their pick. Exits ≈ entries in count; we don't know WHO exits — so no
named exits, ever: the exit liability is shared by all who might exit, carried inside projected
natural decline. Everyone currently in the system stays on the +1/+2 board (more rows than
today); values move with progression or regression. Each year's total board value should be
SIMILAR — not identical, but not collapsing: the continuing players' total decline should equal
what real exits would have removed, and the entrant credit tops the league back up.

## §2.viii — THE ENTRANT SIDE (replaces the §2.i/§2.ii sizing)
The phantom layer carries the FULL expected annual intake (~100 slots): the real pick structure
across all rounds, plus the mechanisms at their measured pick-equivalents (90/92, item 341) —

intake history (smoothed, rule 7), committed and SEALED before render. Flagged `phantom=true`;
never at k=0; never gating a bake.

## §2.ix — ONE CARRIER FOR EXIT RISK (retires §2.iii; prevents the double-count)
The forward decline rate is measured on the WHOLE POPULATION of each historical roster —
INCLUDING the players who then exited (their realized residual paths in full; the
busts-full-weight philosophy, R107.3). A population rate already contains exit risk; therefore
the separate §2.iii retirement haircut RETIRES, folded into the rate (delete, don't disable;
the obituary note rides the implementing build's session). One measured number carries
everything. (The v1.2 seal discipline stands: the population rate is measured ONCE, sealed, and
never iterated against any backtest.)
