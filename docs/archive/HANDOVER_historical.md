## What it is, and what it replaces
The establishment‑P approach charged bust risk TWICE — once in the pick value (`PVC`) and again via the `× P_establish` multiplier — which caused the year‑1/2 cohort CLIFF (cohorts cratering to ~43% of draft capital; the "first‑year sag", old **UNRESOLVED 0c**). Establishment probability returns only as a delayed, position‑clocked **survival‑hold decay** ("no production becomes evidence"), not a day‑one markdown.

Conceptual model (Luke's, affirmed): **value = present production + speculation, where speculation = ceiling × certainty, and certainty resolves up/down as evidence accrues.** Demonstrated > speculative (a proven 90 beats an unproven 75); younger = more runway; "optimism" fades if a promising player stalls (captured by the stage‑bar mechanism — same score vs a rising bar → track falls → value falls). Full design: **`forward_valuation/FORWARD_VALUATION_SPEC.md` (v5).**

## Components (all calibrated, all in the prototype)
1. **ANCHOR** = `PVC[effpk] × TILT[pos]`. TILT (position NPV‑return per pick, centred at pop mean): RUC 1.43, MID 1.16, KEY_DEF 1.00, GEN_FWD 0.93, KEY_FWD 0.86, GEN_DEF 0.76.
2. **PERFORMANCE update** (sample exists): `track` = games‑weighted `(avg − expected_c(pos,pick,season))` measured against the **STAGE bar** (not the eventual‑peak bar — critical); `ρ = games/(games+K)` position clock, K: MID 12 / GEN 16 / KEY 28 / RUC 40 (ruck K high because ruck yr1 form AUC=0.41, below a coin flip — do NOT lower it); `peak_est = basepk + ρ·asym(track)`; `value = anchor · cv(peak_est)/cv(basepk)`. **asym is RELIABILITY‑FADED** (×0.7 above / ×1.25 below at low ρ → ×1.0 both as ρ→1) — essential, and it is ALSO how pedigree fades as evidence accrues (at high ρ the demonstrated record dominates).
3. **SURVIVAL‑HOLD** (no sample): `anchor · SURV[band][year] · POSADJ[pos][year]`, **blended** with the performance path by arrival weight `aw = clip(total_eff_games/15, 0, 1)` (so a partial season doesn't jump the value).

### ⭐ E. CE / VOR CONCEPTUAL LEARNINGS (the groundwork behind the M4 recommendation)
- **CE_α = (mean(x^α))^(1/α)**, monotone-increasing in α; for α<1 it sits BELOW the arithmetic mean. **CE.5 is NOT the median** — it's a *downside-weighted* mean (a single 0 drags it hard; the median ignores one outlier). Lower α weights the **busts**, higher α lets the **stars** pull it toward the mean. So to reward the ceiling you go UP (CE.7), not down to .5 (corrects an easy intuition-trap). At the reliable top α barely matters (outcomes clustered).
- **The backward pool value IS a VOR (value-over-replacement) system:** `posval(best2 + capt_prem − REPL)`. VOR **pre-amplifies stars**: 81 vs 85 avg at REPL 80.1 = 0.9 vs 4.9 VOR = **5.4×** from a 5% output edge. The **softplus `posval` COMPRESSES near replacement** (posval(0.9)=2.56 vs posval(4.9)=5.44 = 2.1×; posval(0)≈2 = a cushion), is **LINEAR well above** replacement, and the **captaincy premium makes it SUPER-LINEAR at the elite top** (avg 120 → 45, above its raw VOR of 40). So the value scale is convex at both ends, linear in the middle.
- **CE leverage is far bigger on the VOR scale than on raw averages** (same 5-player pool: .5→.8 swing = 1.01× on raw avgs, **1.32× on posval-VOR**) — because VOR blows up the relative spread (a star is ~12× a fringe player vs ~1.4× in raw output).
- **The key design implication:** the VOR/captaincy scale ALREADY does most of the ceiling-rewarding, *before* α touches anything. So α should LEAN LOWER, not higher — on a VOR scale the arithmetic mean is essentially "the stars ÷ n". This is what reconciles the tiered α (next).
- **Where α actually bites (numbers):** 7-9 band (93% hit) — top 5% of players carry just **15%** of the value, mean only 1.16× above CE.8 → α nearly moot. 71-99 band (62% hit) — top 5% carry **59%**, mean 1.60× above CE.8 → α decides the number. So α matters at the bust-heavy cheap end, not the reliable top.

**THE DECISION (what we're building):** replace the establishment-P machinery with one **smooth, position-aware surface that UPDATES the pick's prior**, continuous from zero games up. Five concrete changes, all agreed with Luke:

1. **Smooth surface, not a hand-banded grid.** Establishment rate as a smooth monotone function of `log(cumulative games + 1)` and `year-ordinal`, fit per position on the mature cohort (debut ≤ 2019). Replaces the coarse 4-bucket games grid (0 / 1-15 / 16-40 / 41+). WHY: the per-game curves show a *gradual* rise (GEN yr1 ≈ 50→65→80→94% across 2→8→12→16 games), not the 2-step the coarse grid imposed — the coarse buckets threw away the real gradient and produced the 0.65/0.90 clustering Luke spotted. 2. **Fix the off-by-one debut clock.** Current code uses `S = 2026 − debut` for the seasons-row lookup → maps a 2nd-year to the yr1 row, 3rd-year to yr2, etc. (only true first-years are correct, by the clamp). Correct ordinal **`Y = season − debut + 1`**. MEASURED IMPACT: of 252 P-active players, **221 are mis-clocked; 161 move ≥ 0.02 when fixed, mean −0.12 (almost all DOWN)** — the bug systematically over-credits multi-season stallers (Ollie Lord 0.65→0.19, Reef McInnes 0.65→0.19, Ned Moyle 0.69→0.35, debut-2023 GENs 0.69→0.38). 3. **P is continuous from 0 games up; the prospect-path AND credibility-blend are RETIRED (both were stop-gaps).** Zero games is the *floor of the spectrum*, not a protected category. Anyone *in their opportunity window* (debut season or later) sits on the surface, 0 games included, at the empirical 0g rate (GEN 37% yr1 / 21% yr2 / 11% yr3 …). The ONLY players who keep pure pedigree (P inert) are the genuinely **pre-debut** (drafted, season not started — "a kid who's played nothing because the season hasn't started is just his pick"). The current **prospect path** (0–1 games → pure pedigree) is *perverse*: it values Patterson (0g) and Annable (1g) at full pedigree while Cumming (7g, the *most* likely to establish) gets discounted — least-playing protected above most-playing. Continuous spectrum fixes it: **Patterson 0.37 < Annable 0.59 < Cumming 0.77** (year-end). The **credibility blend** (`cred·cell + (1−cred)·prior`) is also retired — its two jobs are absorbed: *stability* by the smoothing (denoises thin cells by borrowing strength), *pedigree-retention* by anchoring the surface to the prior (#4).

4. **The surface UPDATES the pick's prior — pick is NEVER folded into the surface.** Establishment likelihood is **already in the pick-value curve** (a pick 5 is worth more than a pick 50 partly *because* it establishes more often), and early-career value rides mostly on that pick value, so pedigree is priced ONCE, via the pick. The surface's only job is the **per-player update**: ahead of or behind the pace his pick implied, from games + years. Form: **`P = (pick/pathway prior) × (games-year-position update)`**. Confirmed empirically that pick predicts establishment beyond games (top-pick 1-15g yr1 = 80% vs late = 55%; logistic +0.34/−0.23 after controlling for games) — but that is **carried by the prior the surface scales, NOT a pick term in the surface** (a pick term double-counts, since the value anchor already divides by the pick prior). Anchoring as an update also fixes a latent bug under the conditional anchor: a *generic* 0.37 at 0g ÷ a pick-specific prior would make LATE picks perversely keep more of their PVC; anchoring to the prior makes it cancel (0 games, nothing new → value = PVC).

5. **KEY & RUC keep their OWN curves — NOT a shifted GEN.** Shapes are genuinely distinct (per-position logistic: GEN `log(G)` slope **+1.68** / yr **−0.50**; KEY **+1.58 / −0.57**; RUC **+1.34 / −0.53** — games matter *less* for a slow-developing ruck). Implementation: **own surface, partially pooled toward GEN** in proportion to sample thinness (KEY n ≈ 2260 keeps most of its shape; RUC n ≈ 700 leans on GEN's backbone on **coarser bands**, but its strong own anchors — 0g, 41+ — still drive it). ⚠️ The **"−1.4 / −1.5-year runway" is a DIAGNOSTIC, NOT a mechanism — do NOT implement it as a literal year-shift.** A constant shift (a) sends a yr1 KEY to "GEN year −0.42" (extrapolation past all data), and (b) is the wrong *form*: the GEN→KEY gap isn't constant — it's +10 at 0g yr1 and **inverts to −2 by yr4** (a big man with 0 games by year 4 is *worse* off than a midfielder; the patience has expired). The own (pooled) curve captures favourable-early / harsh-once-stalled automatically; a shift never could. (The 1.42 / 1.48 = pooled position-offset ÷ year-coef — a *summary* of how their curve sits vs GEN's, for intuition only.)

**DEFERRED — explicitly NOT now (Luke: nail P first, reward prospects next):** how to **redistribute the value P strips from fresh-cohort prospects**. **⚡ OPEN / UNSOLVED — written down because they need addressing (detail in UNRESOLVED.md):**
- **The asymmetry: personalised RISK, population REWARD.** P (durability) personalises fast from games, but the conditional CEILING (upside) stays at population pedigree because its response to demonstrated scoring is throttled to near-zero for young players: `wdem = games/130`, so a 6-game kid scoring 20 pts above cohort lifts his ceiling by **0.9 points** (Cumming 7g scoring 61 vs basepk 93 → ceiling 92, essentially population). We price personalised downside but population upside; performance quality barely registers until ~40+ games. FIX = personalise the ceiling from demonstrated scoring at a credibility-appropriate rate (the same ~25-game discipline as the P surface), symmetric with P — early-elite rise, early-poor fall, two-sided, sample-disciplined. NOT manufactured convexity — just making the *level* machinery as responsive to evidence as the *probability* machinery.
- **The anchor double-counts the bust risk (the conditional-anchor fix).** The pedigree track anchors on `PVC` (the *unconditional* pick value, which already bakes in the bust rate) then ×P — charging the bust risk twice, which is WHY the cohort sags below the picks. Fix: lift the anchor to the **conditional-on-establishing** value (`≈ PVC / P_prior`, or empirically the realised value of establishers at that pick — as the MSD/SSP re-frame already does), THEN × personalised P. ## 6. MODEL INTERNALS (rl_model.py)
**Currency = SCAR** (SuperCoach-Above-Replacement, season-equivalent), anchored to current-season production vs a position replacement bar. BASE_REF=2026, mid-season. Frozen 2026 SCALE + PVC (pick-value curve).

**6-bucket positions (GRP):** `KEY_DEF, GEN_DEF, MID, GEN_FWD, KEY_FWD, RUC`. Each player has `pos` (settled/future bucket — drives PEAK_AGE, trajectory, key-premium, runway) and a `fut` future-split. `gfut(p)` = settled future position; `bnow(p)` = present position.

**Value flow:** `_vpt = value(p,'bal')` (point value) → present `_v = proj_value(p,0)` (convex) → `_cvx = _v/_vpt` exported (clamped to [1.0, CVX_CAP]).

**`value(p,lens)` (line ~404):** uses `g=gfut(p)` (settled future position) for the curve/pedigree; builds the multi-year product via `proj_from_peak`. Replacement bar `REPL` (line 219): **v3.3 SHIPPED set = MID 80.1, GEN_DEF 78.3, RUC 78.5, KEY_DEF 68.4, GEN_FWD 70.9, KEY_FWD 67.8** (derived via `rl_replacement_derive.py`: Rule-1 pool, kfru 0.5, GDEF/MID 50/50 @ slots 4.16/5.20, KDEF@2.0, GFWD@4.0, KFWD@2.0, RUC@1.64). Softplus value-above-replacement `posval` S_SH=3.0. Captaincy premium above CAPT_THRESH=108 (`capt_bonus`).

**`proj_from_peak(g,lp,a,cur,lens,g0,fut)` (line 231):** `g`=future pos (peak/trajectory/runway/key-premium); `g0`=year-0 (present) pos for REPL (line 244); `fut`=year≥1 REPL blend (line 245). **Residual to fix:** on the +1/+2 toggle, year-0 still uses present-pos g0 instead of rolling to the future pos — slightly inflates forward value (PARKED item 4).

**`level_demo(p)` (lines 147-169) — THE regression mechanism (central to the breakout item):**
demonstrated level = `conf·latest + (1−conf)·baseline`.
- `conf` (trust in the latest season) is capped by `SPIKE_CAP` (default **0.83**, KEY_DEF 0.60). When improving: `conf = (last_games/16)·(1+growth/40)`, capped.
- `baseline` (proven & not old) = `0.5·prior + 0.5·median(last 4 seasons)`. `prior` = recency-weighted (0.60^yr decay, so ancient seasons already fade to ~zero) avg of all-but-last. `proven` = ≥4 seasons with ≥10g.
- So the late-breakout under-valuation is driven by the **0.83 cap + the last-4-median baseline** (which includes a player's climbing years), NOT by ancient form. Lever = make the cap/baseline relax for SUSTAINED multi-season runs. **B1 SHIPPED (v2.6):** now live in `level_demo` (~line 164) — when the last 3 seasons ALL exceed the player's early level by ≥1.4× (`_run`/`_op`/`_rm`), the baseline is raised to the sustained-run mean. Lifts Richards/Xerri/Pickett/Ash/Blakey +~19; steady stars untouched. PARITY-SAFE (flows through the embedded level — no JS port).

**`_dev_advance(L,p)` (line 175):** rolls demonstrated form along the dev curve; games-weight `w=clamp(games/130,0.30,0.85)`. `level_now(p)` = `_LEVEL_OVR` if set else `_dev_advance(level_demo(p),p)`.

**Convexity / option value (`proj_value`, line 473):** Gauss-Hermite integrates `value()` over present-LEVEL uncertainty. width `s = _cov(p)·√var·_upside_w(p)·mu`, `var=PRESENT_VAR` at off=0; `_upside_w = clamp((31−age)/12,0,1)` (1.0@19, 0.5@25, 0 by ~31); `mu = _dev_advance(level_demo(p),p)` (None → no convexity → returns point value). off=0 clamped `[pt, pt·CVX_CAP]` then floored `r=max(r,round(pt))` → cvx≥1.0. **Empirical shape (verified):** premium peaks at ~75–85 output × age 24–27 (≈1.05–1.06); ≈0 at the very bottom (cloud too narrow) and very top (curve near-linear; 115+ ≈1.001). See §14.

**KEY dials:** `PRESENT_VAR=0.25`, `CVX_CAP=1.25`, `SPIKE_CAP={'KEY_DEF':0.60}` (default 0.83), intake 105000.

**B2 current-season unavailability haircut (SHIPPED v2.6):** established players (≥3 pre-2026 seasons, recent peak ≥90) with 0 games in 2026 and season >⅓ done take a banded present-component haircut — **<27: 8.8%, 27–29: 3.9%, 30+: 0** (age curve already prices 30+ decline). Stored per-player as `_b2hc`; applied at the k=0 term of `proj_from_peak` AND `prod_floor`, GUARDED by `BASE_REF==AGE_REF==2026` so it hits the **Now board only** (±boards roll the player forward to where they've returned). Transient — auto-reverses once real 2026 games arrive. **Ported to BOTH JS engines** (`projFromPeak` gains a `preHc` param; `valuePlayer` passes `p.b2hc`; `prodFloor` applies it). Currently flags Tom Green & Nic Martin only.

**Active pool filter `active(p)`:** excludes pos∉GRP, `_retired`, delisted (`_last_listed<2026`); includes `_unplayed`/`_force_active`; unplayed ND/RD draftees 2024+ with 0 games get carried as Now-board SHELLS (3-years-no-games = bust cutoff).

**Pedigree floor:** unplayed / 0-game prospects value at decayed pick pedigree (a MEAN — no convexity premium; pure prospects get cvx=1.0).

## THE PINING INTERSECTIONAL FINDINGS (the analysis that preceded the v4 realisation — still true, now KNOWN to be carried by v4)
- **Crossover is real and CORRECT, not a bug:** a developer playing badly (<~70% of replacement) ends up BELOW a piner (eventual useful 1.0 vs 2.4). Demonstrated sub-par ability IS a worse signal than absence of games (negative evidence vs preserved optionality / raw-upside / team-logjam). The crossover MOVES WITH PEDIGREE: ~80% of repl for high picks (piner worth 3.5), ~63% for low picks (piner worth 1.8). v4 reproduces this via its form features — no enforced floor needed (below the line developers SHOULD sit lower).
- **Position is the lens pick alone hides:** pining RATE — RUC/KEY_DEF/KEY_FWD ~32-34% (normal, slow develop), MID 21% / GEN_DEF 23% / GEN_FWD 17% (rarer). Pining RECOVERY (useful-val / P(useful) / P(gun)) — **RUC 7.9/40%/24% (pining nearly FREE, true slow-burn)**, KEY_DEF 3.0/24%/4%, MID 3.0/20%/10% (the Shuey star-tail), GEN_FWD 2.0/18%/2%, GEN_DEF 1.7/14%/5%, KEY_FWD 1.6/26%/2% (reach repl but rarely excel — role players). **Position sets the decay RATE; pick sets the FLOOR height.** v4 carries both.
- **High-pick piners who became great are MANY** (Shuey 103, Hogan 90, Anderson 91, McKay 83, Lynch, Setterfield, Doedee, Tarrant, Reid…) — 44 pick-1-20s played ≤8 games in their first 2 yrs; a big slice recovered, mostly key-position/injury-delayed. **INJURY CONFOUND is severe: 87/236 (37%) of high picks had <6g in BOTH yr1-2 then a 10+g season later** — cannot separate "0 games injured" from "0 games not good enough." This is why high-pick pining must never be crushed; v4's gentle treatment + the distribution tail (for display) preserve the exceptions.
