## §1 — FILED AT v101
**R100.11 — THE ABSENCE PENALTY FADES ON EVIDENCE, NOT ON A CLOCK. OWNER-RULED 2026-07-14** (*"Agree on
Smith — evidence-fade"*).
**THE CASE — PR #82's own return flagged it:** **Bailey Smith missed 2024, returned and averaged 116 (2025)
then 122 (2026) — and was still docked −515 SCAR**, because the build's declared fade was a fixed
two-post-return-season SCHEDULE and he was only halfway through it. **He had already produced the evidence
that refutes the prior, and a clock overrode it.**
**THE RULE:** the absence term is a **PRIOR about an UNOBSERVED return**, and **EVIDENCE DISSOLVES IT IN
BOTH DIRECTIONS.** He returns and plays well: the prior was wrong — drop it. He returns and plays badly:
that is **ALREADY IN HIS LEVEL** — his production IS the bad news, and charging the absence on top is a
second bite at the same fact, a DOUBLE-CHARGE. The fade therefore runs on **EVIDENCE WEIGHT — games played
since return — not seasons elapsed**, and a returner producing at or above his pre-absence level **sheds it
entirely.**
**NO SCHEDULE FALLBACK — and the reason matters:** the one honest argument for a clock (a man who missed a
year with a knee is likelier to miss again, and good football does not disprove that) is TRUE — **and it is
an AVAILABILITY risk, not a LEVEL risk.** *Will he play* is a different question from *how good is he when
he plays*, and the engine already has an availability layer (`_avail_hc` · the LTI register · D2's R5).
**Welding a schedule into the level term would charge the fragility TWICE.**
**AND IT IS THE MECHANISM THIS CHAPTER EXISTS TO KILL:** `S_AGE`'s zero at 30 · the pedigree's cliff at four
seasons · the 12-game bar · the 5.0-point bar — **every one a SCHEDULE deciding what only EVIDENCE should
decide.** A two-season fade on Bailey Smith is the same rule in a new coat.
**⚠ TWO ASSUMPTIONS PR #82 DECLARED AND NOBODY HAS RULED — STILL OPEN:**
1. Multiple / longer absences are charged **ONCE**, on the most recent return, and are **NOT scaled by gap
   length** — should a three-year absence cost more than a one-year? Intuitively yes; currently it does not.
   **A MAGNITUDE question, separate from the fade.**
2. The measured U curve is **clamped to zero below age ~20.**
**DISPOSITION:** PR #82 carries the schedule fade ⇒ **REWORK REQUIRED before any bake.** Item 111's
prescreen (seat 6's) stands as recorded; **the Fable seat re-verifies it independently from PR #82's
committed artifacts before any rung advances** (owner-instructed 2026-07-15: the Opus seats' judgement is
not ground truth).
