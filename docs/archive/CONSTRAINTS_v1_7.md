## PART 1 — GUARDS

### G-COHORT — cohort no-arbitrage bound — BINDING (canonical text = the owner's wording, re-confirmed 2026-07-08; WD-1 + WD-1b closed; UPHELD against a failing candidate same day)
"For each draft class, take the total value of that class's players in a given career year (the
class-year sum). Average those sums across the sampled classes to get one figure per career year. Each of
the year-4, year-5, and year-6 figures must be ≤130% (hard; guide 120–125%) of the denominator — the
LOWER of the year-1 and year-2 figures — measured walk-forward, leak-free. Class sizes differ by design:
attrition leaves the later-year total shared among far fewer players while the year-1 total is split among
30–60% more, so the bound deliberately caps how much survivor boom the model prices relative to what it
pays the whole incoming class. A breach means year-1 boom potential is underpriced — the fix is raising
the year-1 side, never cutting survivors or young." (Owner-worded; denominator = min(year-1, year-2),
owner-picked 2026-07-08.) Cohort coherence governs; per-player calibration is a subordinate flag.
· EACH-YEAR, not the 4-6 average: years 4, 5, and 6 are tested individually — tighter than the superseded
  "avg-year-4-6" wording.
· OWNER RATIONALE (re-affirmed 2026-07-08, upholding the guard against the W4 candidate's measured
  breach, owner's words): "If year 1-2 players were going to be worth 50% more 2-3 years later, one would
  simply hold them in a market economy, and as a result, the value of younger players would rise to
  compensate."
· REMEDIATION DOCTRINE (owner-ruled 2026-07-08): "The solution is not to blanket increase the value of
  younger players to tick a box, it is to investigate why younger players are not receiving the
  appropriate value they deserve relative to the players they become 3-4 years later and rectify it."
  Blanket young multipliers are FORBIDDEN as a fix; the year-1 side rises via an identified, quantified
  mechanism correction. (The retired "1.19× uniform sit-out lift" idea stays retired.)
· NOT PER-CAPITA (obituary): the retired supervisor's supplement carried "per-capita average" as the
  precise owner constraint — OVERRULED by the owner's worked example (2026-07-08). Under class sums with
  attrition, this bound is far STRONGER per survivor than a per-capita 125% would be. Do not "correct" the
  construction back.
· FITTED-BOARD TRAP: a computation run on the FITTED present board is invalid as evidence — the model
  ingested those cohorts' outcomes (leak-flattered). The basis is asserted by READING THE SCRIPT'S CODE
  (the model scoring cohort year T must see only data ≤ T), and the construction is asserted the same way
  (class-year SUMS; each of years 4/5/6; denominator = min(y1, y2)). Any ratio quoted without naming the
  walk-forward book AND the class-sum construction is rejected. NUMERIC FINGERPRINT RETIRED (obituary,
  2026-07-08): the old aid (avg(4-6)≈139 vs avg(1-2)≈111, ratio ≈1.25, from the v2.5 B1 index) was shown
  by the cold audit to numerically EQUAL the genuine walk-forward B1 row (138.9/111.0) — it discriminates
  nothing; do not resurrect it. Code reading is the only basis test. (Trap provenance: retired-supervisor
  supplement ①, PRE-W4 — its construction wording there is the overruled per-capita one; only the trap
  logic carries.)
· History: manifest v1 said "year-1 only" — drift; final owner wording (WD-1b): the LOWER of year-1/year-2.
· Provenance: owner ground truth (the turn-one "loop"); construction owner-worded 2026-07-08, re-confirmed
  in the owner's own words during the v4.1 remediation, and UPHELD (with the rationale above) against the
  audited W4 breach the same day.
