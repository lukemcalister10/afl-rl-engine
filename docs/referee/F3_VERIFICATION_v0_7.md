2. RESOLVED — Early-fold tuning is now origin-safe (§2.3). The unsafe nearest-fold copy has been removed. Where no target-safe inner cutoff exists, every capacity dial uses a predeclared finite-grid median, and the scorecard identifies the fold as DEFAULT-TUNED. The protocol expressly prohibits importing tuning from any other outer fold. This closes the future-information route identified in v0.5.

3. NEW ISSUE INTRODUCED — BLOCKER. The confirmation vault is touched once physically, but used adaptively statistically (§7.12). The original defect is resolved in substance: failed ingredients are stripped; a rebuild that fails confirmation produces no adoption candidate; provisional peak units are barred from adoption; confirmation consequences are now explicit. The new strip procedure, however, uses the same confirmation outcomes twice: (1) ingredient-ablation results determine which subset of units is stripped; (2) the resulting data-selected core is then tested against C0 on those same confirmation folds. Precomputing every possible strip branch in one sealed batch prevents a second file access. It does not make the selected branch independent of the confirmation results. The final "confirmed core" is selected using the data on which its dispositive superiority is then assessed. This recreates adaptive reuse inside the vault itself. What resolves it: Either reserve untouched evidence for the post-strip core, or define and calibrate the entire strip-and-retest procedure as one composite statistical test. Merely enumerating all branches before execution is insufficient.

4. NEW ISSUE INTRODUCED — BLOCKER. Confirmation-branch multiplicity is not controlled (§§7.5, 7.7, 7.9, 7.12). The v0.5 selection-era multiplicity defects are substantially fixed: selection is now expressly exploratory; each candidate contributes one test per declared door; removal tests are counted; removal tests use the same adjusted αr at which they are counted; §7.9 no longer represents the exploratory regime as the binding inferential claim. The binding confirmation event introduces a new uncorrected family. With K units, the strip rule can select among as many as 2^K possible post-strip cores. All branches may be computed, but the branch ultimately reported is chosen from confirmation outcomes. No multiplicity adjustment or calibrated composite null covers: the data-selected strip branch; its final comparison with C0; or the interaction between the unit-level tests and final-core test. Section 7.12(5) also says the confirmed core must "resolvedly" beat C0 without freezing the exact level applicable to that post-strip comparison. What resolves it: Freeze a valid gatekeeping or closed-testing procedure for the complete confirmation decision tree, including the final post-strip core claim and its exact error level.

5. RESOLVED — W3 predicted production is uniquely defined (§4, Appendix B.1). The emission contract is now a per-season (P, G, μ) triple. W3 production is exactly Σ P_s G_s μ_s / Σ P_s G_s. The zero-denominator rule and ranking treatment are fixed, and Appendix B.1 distinguishes the chosen functional from the rejected alternatives. Two conforming implementations now have a unique M4 input.

6. NEW ISSUE INTRODUCED — AMEND-BEFORE-FREEZE. The M8 metric is repaired, but peak-door confirmation tests the wrong door (§4 M8, §7.12). The original remote-peak exploit is mechanically closed. M8 now reach-weights each seasonal conditional mean by the probability that it is the highest-ranked reachable season, conditional on surviving somewhere in W6. Appendix B.2 discriminates it from the former naïve maximum. The new problem is downstream. Section 7.12(2) confirms units only when their removal harms the headline, which is M4. Section 7.12(4) says provisional M8 units later "confirm or strip by rule (2)." A mechanism admitted specifically because it improves M8 but not M4 will therefore be stripped at confirmation even when its M8 gain reproduces perfectly. In effect, the protocol opens an M8 selection door but supplies only an M4 adoption door. That is inconsistent with the owner-ruled peak door encoded in §§4 and 7.5. What resolves it: Door-matched confirmation. An M4 admission must confirm on M4; an M8 admission must confirm on M8 when extension folds make it measurable, with the resulting confirmation family predeclared and controlled. Note: The phrase "exact expected realised peak" remains stronger than the emission contract technically permits; the formula is the expected maximum of emitted conditional means under the frozen independence convention. The formula itself is nevertheless exact and uniquely implementable.

7. NOT RESOLVED — BLOCKER. The sole binding confidence interval still omits the principal time dependence (§4 uncertainty, §7.12). The protocol now honestly labels the limitation: player-clustered intervals capture player-sampling uncertainty but not common season or cutoff shocks. It also correctly labels the cutoff sign screen and leave-one-out results as descriptive rather than coverage corrections. That honesty does not validate the sole binding test. The confirmation claim still relies on the same player-clustered bootstrap, although: W3 confirmation contains only three cutoffs, 2020–2022; their outcome windows overlap heavily; all players within a cutoff share common football seasons and system-wide shocks; and the protocol's object is future-period performance, not merely resampling players inside three fixed historical windows. A fresh holdout protects against adaptive model selection. It does not make player resampling an uncertainty model for future-season generalisation. The N=200 null calibration applies to the selection pipeline and tests player-bootstrap behaviour under artificial perturbations. It does not calibrate the dispositive confirmation statistic against temporal dependence. What resolves it: Either provide a binding inference procedure whose sampling unit reflects future periods, or narrow the dispositive claim explicitly to performance on the fixed confirmation-era player population and remove any inferential claim about future-season generalisation. The latter would materially narrow what "confirmed" means.

10. NOT RESOLVED — BLOCKER; 10b separately resolved (§6). 10b — RESOLVED: The acceptance twin now permits a discontinuity only when registered before scoring and measured and reported as a margin. This matches the protocol's declared-exception path. 10a — NOT RESOLVED: C0-smooth is described as a natural cubic spline; with four interior knots {21, 24, 27, 30}; boundary knots {18, 33}; and "exactly four free coefficients." Those statements do not define one standard natural-spline basis. A natural cubic spline with four interior knots ordinarily has more than four free basis coefficients after the natural-boundary constraints. "Exactly four" therefore requires additional basis restrictions that are not specified. The text also does not define the response being regressed in the claimed closed-form weighted least-squares fit — whether the drift is fit to next-season change, a residual after the recency component, or a joint design matrix with that component. Two implementations can still differ while claiming compliance. What resolves it: Supply the exact design matrix or basis-construction formula, coefficient ordering, fitting response and joint relationship with the recency term, plus a numerical fixture.

11. NOT RESOLVED — BLOCKER. C3 is mostly frozen, but cannot execute as written on early folds (§§2.3, 8.1). The LightGBM version, exact parameter names, objectives, grids, iteration cap, tie-breaks, missing handling, category feature and determinism settings are now substantially complete. The remaining contradiction is early stopping. Section 8.1 requires early stopping on a §2.3 inner-eligible validation set. For early outer folds, no such set exists: W1 at cutoff 2009 has no inner cutoff u ≤ 2008; W3 has none through cutoff 2011; W6 has none through cutoff 2014. Section 2.3 gives capacity-dial defaults when there is no inner cutoff, but it does not state how LightGBM's early stopping operates without a validation set. early_stopping_rounds=50 cannot be applied to an absent validation set, and no default tree count replaces it. Different implementations could use 5,000 trees, omit those folds or create a non-protocol validation split. What resolves it: Freeze an explicit no-inner-validation C3 rule, including the exact number of boosting rounds and hyperparameter defaults for those folds.

13. RESOLVED — Position mapping and TALL-DEV disposition (§5.1). The raw vocabulary and mapping are now in the protocol itself. Unexpected values HALT. Historical position uses the origin-safe drafted position, current-era position fields are banned, missing positions are counted, and TALL-DEV is precisely defined. The former parent-population substitution is removed. If TALL-DEV is unmeasurable for a required admission statistic, admission is blocked unless the owner gives a specific recorded waiver. This is transparent and mechanically enforceable.

14. RESOLVED — Only current-core tests discharge current rows (§7.8). A resolved interaction row may now be discharged only by a bundle tested against the current core. Tests against every earlier version explicitly lapse. The stale-test route is closed.

15. NOT RESOLVED — BLOCKER. REF-FADE contradicts the binding "never below" rule (§7.10). The protocol now measures a floor and emits numerical predicates and margins. That is a material improvement. The enacted test nevertheless does not enforce L-SAGE-FADE as written. The governing twin says veteran value must decline toward the measured floor, never below it and never be propped above evidence. REF-FADE instead permits the pooled mean prediction for players aged 33+ to fall as low as 85% of the floor, and permits it to sit as high as 115% of the floor. It also tests a pooled mean rather than preventing individual veteran forecasts from falling below the floor. Further, F-1 checks only the fitted age effect, not the candidate's total veteran prediction. Other model components may offset it and produce an increasing total forecast with age while REF-FADE still passes. The ±15% rule is therefore not a measurement margin around "never below"; it changes the substantive predicate. What resolves it: Either enforce the binding rule on total fitted predictions without permitting below-floor values, or obtain an owner-worded amendment to L-SAGE-FADE defining a pooled tolerance band and its intended scope.

16. RESOLVED — M5 and D3 are uniquely specified (§4 M5, §5.2 D3, Appendix B.3). M5 now specifies: a six-season truncation; realised and predicted total formulas; level and standing populations; weights; and cutoff aggregation. D3 now defines the jointly refitted removal comparison, sign, statistic, paired interval, per-lens treatment and duplicate-input identity fixture. The prior implementation ambiguity is closed.

Additional note — stale twin status language. The actual acceptance twin has been amended, so F2b is substantively closed. The protocol still describes the amendment as merely "WORDED," presents old text as CURRENT and amended text as PROPOSED, and says the exception remains inactive until filing. That is now factually stale relative to the governing file. It should be conformed before freeze, although it does not revive finding 10b.

F1 status. The §11 cross-references remain true at their cited sections. F1 remains PASS. The acceptance amendment also means the Law-3 map line no longer points into a protocol/twin contradiction.

VERDICT: FAIL
v0.7 closes a majority of the concrete specification gaps and represents a substantial improvement over v0.5. It should nevertheless not proceed to F4 freeze in its present form. The remaining defects affect the validity or uniqueness of load-bearing adjudication: (1) confirmation outcomes select the stripped core subsequently tested on those same outcomes; (2) that adaptive confirmation branch family is not statistically controlled; (3) the sole binding CI does not represent future-period dependence; (4) C0-smooth remains internally underdefined; (5) C3 cannot early-stop on folds with no eligible inner validation set; and (6) REF-FADE permits outcomes the binding age-fade law expressly forbids. The owner retains the freeze decision.
