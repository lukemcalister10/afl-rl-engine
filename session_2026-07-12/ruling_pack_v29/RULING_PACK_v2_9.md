## DECISION 3 — THE YEAR-ZERO LAW: ratifying your rule [G-Y0 guard]

**The candidate text (your words, drafted T75, filed PROPOSED/PENDING-OWNER in CONSTRAINTS v1.7 —
your pen ratifies):**

> "Picks are year 0 of the cohort curve. At each pick (band), the draft-composition-weighted
> average of position-conditioned V0 must equal the derived PVC within [tolerance — owner sets];
> per-position deviations are permitted and must net to zero. The class's year-0 pick-sum,
> week-one cohort sum, and year-1/2 sums chain with bounded steps, measured walk-forward. Breach
> repair: raise the young side; never cut survivors; never bend the derived curve."

**The rationale, beside it (the record, T74–T75):** this is your no-arbitrage principle extended
backward to draft night — if a pick's price and the day-one price of the kids it converts into
diverge, one of them is wrong, and a market would close the gap. Position conditioning may
REDISTRIBUTE value at a pick (a defender-heavy pick mix starting lower, a mid-heavy mix higher) —
it may never LEAK it. The supervisor's assessment on the record: the most economically coherent
idea in the project; any gap must earn its size.

**What binding it DOES constrain:** adds one identity check and one chain check per band to the
measured guard suite, walk-forward, alongside the cohort guard it extends (G-COHORT grows
backward to year 0). On breach it forces a repair job on the YOUNG side only, protected by the
same three prohibitions as Decision 2.

**What binding it does NOT constrain:** any individual player's price (it is a band-aggregate
law); per-position deviations (free, provided they net); the tolerance (yours to set, and
adjustable by your ruling); pickless pathway entrants (SSP/international/etc. price off the
pathway pedestal [PICKEQ], outside the pick identity — Decision 4 is not gated by this law); and
it moves NO value by itself — it is a test, and repairs are separate owner-ruled levers.

**Your ratification options, symmetric:**
- **(i) Ratify BINDING now**, tolerance set today. Consequence: given the illustration, the first
  seam table likely breaches bands 1–45 at −9…−14% under a derived-curve world — binding now is a
  commitment to a young-side level-repair job inside v2.9. Cleanest law; largest immediate
  workload.
- **(ii) Ratify ADVISORY first** — the v2.9 seam table is measured and scored against it, you set
  the tolerance on sight of real numbers, and it converts to BINDING at the following seam.
  Consequence: no forced repair this batch; the law's teeth arrive one bake later. (This mirrors
  how G-CONVEX is being handled — advisory until your explicit word.)
- **(iii) Decline for now** — it stays PROPOSED in the registry. Consequence: the seam table is
  still delivered (Decision 2 stands on its own), but nothing polices the identity going forward;
  the year-0 end of the cohort curve remains the one unguarded end.
A note either way: the tolerance is the law's one free number. The illustration suggests
deviations of −9…−14% at level today; a tolerance set tighter than that is a scheduled breach,
set looser it is a rubber stamp — the honest move is to set it AFTER the build's real table
(which argues (ii)), or set it now and own the repair job (which is (i)).
